Short Papers & Posters

INDIS 2026 - International Conference on Design & Innovation Studies

Short Papers & Posters provide concise overviews of emerging research, preliminary findings, work-in-progress, and innovative ideas. These submissions are ideal for early-stage research or provocative concepts that warrant discussion with the design community.

Note: Accepted short papers will be presented as poster sessions during the conference.

Typical Structure

  1. Title and Abstract (100–150 words)
  2. Keywords (3–5)
  3. Introduction and Problem Statement
  4. Research Approach/Methodology
  5. Preliminary Findings or Framework
  6. Discussion and Future Work
  7. References

Length & Requirements

  • Maximum 3 pages including references
  • Clear statement of research question or problem
  • Description of methodology or approach
  • Preliminary findings or conceptual framework
  • Discussion of future work directions

Poster Presentation Guidelines

  1. Poster Size: A0 size (841 × 1189 mm or 33.1 × 46.8 inches) in portrait orientation
  2. Design Guidelines: Clear, readable fonts (minimum 24pt for body text), high contrast colors, logical flow
  3. Content Structure: Title, authors/affiliation, abstract, key findings, visuals, and next steps
  4. Interactive Elements: Prepare to discuss your work during poster sessions

Paper Submission

INDIS 2026 - International Conference on Design & Innovation Studies

All submissions to INDIS 2026 – International Conference on Design and Innovation Studies must be made electronically through the EasyChair conference management system.

Authors are required to submit their manuscripts in PDF format through the official submission portal. Submissions sent by email or other means will not be considered.

The EasyChair submission link will be provided on this page.

Submission Process

Authors should follow the steps below to submit their paper:

  1. Create or log in to an EasyChair account.
  2. Select the appropriate submission category:
    • Full Research Paper
    • Case Study and Industry Paper
    • Research-through-Design Contribution
    • Poster / Short Paper
  3. Enter the required metadata:
    • Paper title
    • Abstract
    • Keywords
    • Author details and affiliations
  4. Upload the manuscript in PDF format.
  5. Confirm and finalize the submission.

Authors may update their submissions any time before the submission deadline.

Submission Requirements

  • Papers must be written in English
  • Manuscripts must be submitted in PDF format
  • Submissions must follow the INDIS 2026 formatting guidelines
  • Maximum 3 pages including references
  • Submissions must be original and unpublished
  • Simultaneous submission to other conferences or journals is not permitted

Review Process

All submissions will undergo peer review by members of the Technical Program Committee and external reviewers.

Review criteria include:

  • Clarity of research question or problem statement
  • Appropriateness of methodology or approach
  • Quality and significance of preliminary findings
  • Potential for future development
  • Contribution to ongoing design discourse

Important Note on Anonymization

Submissions should be prepared for double-blind review.

Authors should ensure that:

  • Author names and affiliations are removed from the manuscript
  • Self-references are anonymized where necessary
  • Document properties do not reveal author identity
  • Visual materials should not contain identifying information

Detailed instructions for preparing anonymized submissions will be provided.

INDIS Publication

INDIS 2026 - International Conference on Design & Innovation Studies

All accepted and presented short papers at INDIS 2026 – International Conference on Design and Innovation Studies will be published in the conference proceedings by Springer, subject to compliance with the publisher's submission and formatting requirements.

Authors of accepted papers will be required to revise their manuscripts in accordance with the Springer proceedings format and submission guidelines, which will be provided at a later stage on the conference website.

Selected short papers may also be considered for extension invitations to the main conference tracks or journal special issues based on the quality of presentation and feedback received during poster sessions.

Important Notes

  • Only registered and presented papers will be included in the Springer proceedings
  • Papers must comply with the Springer formatting requirements (to be updated)
  • Poster presentations are mandatory for all accepted short papers
  • Extension opportunities will be communicated directly to relevant authors
